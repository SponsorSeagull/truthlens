About TruthLens

TruthLens is a fully client-side fake news detection system. All processing — data loading, model training, and inference — runs locally in the browser using Pyodide (Python compiled to WebAssembly via scikit-learn). No data leaves your device and no external prediction APIs are used.

Logistic Regression is a probabilistic linear classifier that outputs class probabilities directly, making confidence scores easy to interpret. It trains quickly on high-dimensional sparse TF-IDF vectors.

SVM (LinearSVC + Platt Scaling) finds the maximum-margin decision boundary in TF-IDF feature space. Probability estimates are obtained via CalibratedClassifierCV with 3-fold cross-validation.

Text Cleaning: Before training and at inference time, news wire datelines (e.g. "WASHINGTON (Reuters) —"), source tags, URLs, and scraping artifacts are stripped so the model learns genuine linguistic patterns rather than source labels — addressing the shortcut learning flaw common in the Kaggle ISOT dataset.

Research Findings

Finding 1 — Shortcut Learning

Without cleaning, the model's strongest predictor was the word "Reuters" (coefficient −11.5), meaning it learned source attribution as a proxy for real news rather than genuine writing patterns. Text cleaning reduced this to −3.8 and shifted the top signal to "said" — an actual journalism pattern.

Finding 2 — Temporal Drift

The dataset covers 2016–2018 US political news. Articles about post-2020 events (COVID, 2026 elections, SpaceX IPO) are misclassified because these topics are entirely absent from training data. This is a known limitation of static NLP classifiers.

Finding 3 — Domain Bias

The model performs well on US political news but poorly on other domains (UK politics, health, sports) because the Kaggle dataset was scraped primarily from US-focused sources like Reuters and PolitiFact.
